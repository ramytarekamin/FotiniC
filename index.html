University of Cincinnati

A philosopher of minds, images, and social worlds.

Fotini Charalabidou is a PhD Candidate in Philosophy and an MA student in Psychology at the University of Cincinnati. Her work brings together philosophy of cognitive science, social philosophy, aesthetics, and phenomenological inquiry, with recurring attention to obsession, categorization, visual media, and the forms of attention that shape experience.

Research

Questions at the border of cognition and culture.

01

Cognitive Science and Mind

Work in philosophy of cognitive science and philosophy of psychology, with interest in embodied cognition, non-linear methods, affect, and the structure of lived experience.

02

Social Philosophy

Inquiry into social categorization, collective obsession, threat, and the fragile but reparable conditions under which shared social worlds take shape.

03

Aesthetics and Attention

Research on aesthetic attention, visual media, religious belief, friendship, and the ways image practices invite, redirect, and discipline perception.
